PREPARATION TIPS TO FACE FLOOD




You need to:


1. Understand flood risk in your area


2. Prepare your home and property


3. Respond when water start to raise


4. Recover after a flood

You can prepare for flood by:


1. Check with your local council about flood plans or records


2. Ask authorities about relocation routes and centres


3. Prepare emergency kits


4. Prepare a household flood plan


5. Keep list of emergency telephone numbers in display

Prepare your property:


1. Secure hazardous items


2. Roll up rugs, move furniture, electrical items
and valuables to a higher level


3. Move your PETS to a safer place


4. Place IMPORTANT documents, valuables and vital medicals supplies into WATERPROOF CASE in an accessible location


5. Monitor forecast and warnings online

Relocating to safer ground:


1. Pack warm clothing, essential medication, valuables and personal papers in WATERPROOF BAGS along with emergency kits


2. Empty freezers and refrigerators, leaving doors open to avoid damage or loss if they loat


3. TURN OFF power, water and gas


4. ALWAYS bring your mobile phone


5. DO NOT drive into water of unknown depth and current

If too LATE to leave:


1. ALWAYS monitor your local radio for warnings, advice and helps


2. Get to higher ground


3. SWITCH OFF electricity and gas supplies


4. Prepare for the well being of pets


5. Check your emergency kits


6. DO NOT allow children to play in or near floodwaters


7. Avoid entering floodwaters. If you must do so, WEAR solid shoes and check depths and current with a stick


8. STAY AWAY from drains, culverts and water over knee deep


9. DO NOT use gas or electrical appliances that have been in floodwater
